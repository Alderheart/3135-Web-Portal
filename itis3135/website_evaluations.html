Website Evaluations

CRAP: Yale School of Art

The official landing page of the Yale School of Art. The
reason this website looks the way it does is because it
can be edited by anyone and everyone.

  • Contrast: Contrast and color theory is essentially nonexistent.
    Randomly chosen colors clash with each other in a way that
    could only happen if this lack of contrast was intended. The gradients
    should be removed and all text should have a darker background.
  • Repetition: The entire page is highly repetitive. Information and
    buttons are laid out in lists with the entire background
    being a repeating looped gif. The background should be removed
    and text could be organized differently.
  • Alignment: There seems to be some semblance of alignment as elements
    are ordered around the central gray block. However, it seems the
    website ran out of content as all of the elemnts are shoved to the left
    side. Instead, elements could be spread out more.
  • Proximity: Proximity is terrible, elements are way to close together,
    and there is a huge chunk of unused space on the left. Like mentioned in
    alignment, text should be spread out more.

GOOD: ServerJars

ServerJars is a third party website that hosts large amounts of mirror
links for Bukkit, Paper, Spigot, and other server files.

  • Contrast: Contrast is good. The website utilizes dark and light areas to
    direct your eyes towards the important information on the page. Intuitive
    direct your eyes towards the important information on the page. Intuitive
    and well designed.
  • Repetition: Repetition is used in a way that is logical and breaks the website
    into sections that are easy to understand at a glance.
  • Alignment: Alignment is great, with elements centered to bring them to your
    attention. Each column represents a category of server files and helps you
    quickly find what you're looking for.
  • Proximity: Proximity is good. Elements are spaced apart so that they don't
    conflict with each other's space yet is cohesive enough that it's obvious
    when something is in the same section.
